# v1 sample run (2026-06-01)

This is real, live data fetched from the NRCS AWDB REST API on 2026-06-01.
The Overpass leg of the run was simulated from bounding-box centroids
because the build environment could not reach overpass-api.de. When you run
`python -m trailsnow.v1 --all` locally you will get the actual Overpass
geometry too (trail names, way IDs, segment lengths).

Format roughly matches what the CLI prints. SNWD is snow depth, WTEQ is snow
water equivalent. Both in inches. "as of" is the latest non-null daily
reading from that station.

## What this tells us about v1

The Cascades seeds work cleanly when a SNOTEL is within ~10 km of the trail
(Paradise, Olallie, Harts Pass). It degrades when the nearest station is
20+ km away and at a meaningfully different elevation (Lake 22, anything in
the Palouse). That's expected and is precisely what v2 (SNODAS grid sampled
along the trail) is designed to fix.

Two early signals that the system works:
1. Paradise (5150 ft) still has 14 in of snow on May 31; Stevens Pass and
   Olallie Meadows (both ~4000 ft) are bare. Snowline today is somewhere
   between 4000 and 5000 ft in the Cascades. That matches the season.
2. The seasonal melt-out is visible at Alpine Meadows: 6 in on May 29 down
   to 5 in on May 31. Real, live data.
